How would you describe your musical background?
Influenced by synth pioneers Laserdance, Jean Michael Jarre, John Carpenter, Fancy and the Spanish italo band Azyl y Negro, Pufferfisk was founded in 2011.

What is your main musical influence and/ or collective?
We are influenced by, and collaborate with, Spanish robots with bad American accents.

Can you describe the main milestones in your earlier production/ projects?
Ten years ago, in 2011 we released “Greetings in Space.” In 2016 we released the EP “Bolero,” with a cover of the track by Fancy. In 2017 our album “Retrofututismo” was released and in 2020 we released the EP “We are Pufferfisk”, as well as the 2020 smash hit “El Rincón del Pollo”, which was selected as one of the best tracks for that year by Samir Yousefi on Swedish National Radio.

Which are your current projects and what inspires you the most at the moment?
We can’t stop making excessive and happy-go-lucky italo disco with 80s retro vibes and tacky visuals.

Which are your greatest passions as an artist?
Synthesizers, 80s TV shows (like Miami Vice and Knight Rider), italo disco and cheesy melodies. Our motto is “More is more”.

What are your ideas behind your contribution to the Nomads in Sound anniversary compilation?
We watched the old TV show Knight Rider and were impressed by David Hasselhoff: his poofy hair, his phenomenal self confidence and his womanizing swagger. So we made the tribute “Hola Mujeres”, complete with Spanish robots singing “El coche del noche. Hola mujeres: ¿cómo estás? Yo me llama El Hasselhof.”
