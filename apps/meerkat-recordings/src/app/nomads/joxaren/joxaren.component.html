How would you describe your musical background?
I make intricate melodic electronica with vague echoes of hip-hop and drum'n'bass under the name Hab. I play guitar and sing in bands, among them a four-piece called Mouth that made it as far as a major-label contract (on Warner subsidiaries Telegram and Reprise in Sweden and America, respectively) before dissolving in 1994 under the weight of an archetypally "corporate rock" experience. I spent subsequent years traveling from Sweden to Paris, and finally to the Hasidic neighborhood of Williamsburg in Brooklyn (site of a sort of late-'90s New York bohemian renaissance), where I recorded most of my material under the name Hab, wich resulted in the album maPOd, released on Dot in 1997.

Can you describe the main milestones in your earlier production/ projects?
The first of the material I produced in Brooklyn Swedish label, later renamed dotshop. Collaborations with then-Dot artist Krister Linder (of Tupilaq); our debut EPs for the label were among its first releases and helped garner Dot the attention that transformed the label into something of an overnight sensation. Later projects include Binger the Voyager with Fredrik Joelson and Kool Kojak /Al D Bomba (CD Sweet Taste of Nothing, Oxygen Music Works 1999) and soundscapes with Khim Efraimsson for the poetry koncept Broken Word / Johannes Anyuru & Mercies May (CD Anatomy of a Dying Star, 2005). I have also made music for the Håkan Lidbo/ radio ström koncepts and compilations (what we are -music for alien civilizations, 2008) and (Precycling Namibia -The Music From The Future, 2010).

Which are your current projects and what inspires you the most at the moment?
My most recent album Hab on Kotangens records(2011) is a collection of both rhythmically stunning and melodically engaging post-techno, interweaving thin, intricately syncopated percussion with thematic figures that are given far more focus and development than is typical of even the most armchair-oriented of electronica. In 2021 I released the track Höst on Meerkat Recordings Nomads in Sound 03.

What are your ideas behind your contribution to the Nomads in Sound anniversary compilation?
With the track Worlds (on fire) I believe HAb wants to convey that not only life on the planet suffers from irresponsible deeds against nature and climate. Intergalactic life, other worlds -- inner and outer – are also affected. In other words, we are causing much more damage than we can even understand.
