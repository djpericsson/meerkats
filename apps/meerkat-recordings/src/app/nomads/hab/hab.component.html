How would you describe your musical background?
Playful, deep, and melodic electronica with vague echoes of hip hop, drum'n'bass, ambient and techno under the name HAb.

I grew up during the 70's glam, punk and synth era and when I got my first guitar at 10, I started writing songs and forming bands. During our teens, we played at the nightclubs in Gothenburg and the surrounding areas.

In the late 80's I ended up in Stockholm after a couple of years of design studies in Paris. Played in different bands and made music with Fredrik Joelson and eventually the indie rock band Mouth was formed (which was signed with Warner/Telegram in Sweden and Reprise in the US).

In the early 90's, my fascination increased for electronically produced music. Inspiration came from the machines, everyday surrealism, art, technology, spirituality, mysticism, fantasy, futurism, club cultures etc. Through exploring electronic frequencies and rhythms I encountered HAb which resulted in the album maPOd, released on Dot 1997.

Can you describe the main milestones in your earlier production/ projects?
I collaborated with Krister Linder/Tupilaq on the track Pilaque on Alaskanlab 1995, Audio pollution (SW), and our debut EPs for Dot were among its first releases and helped garner Dot the attention that transformed the label into something of an overnight sensation. The HAb album MaPOd was released in 1997.

Later projects include 'Binger the Voyager' with Fredrik Joelson and Kool Kojak (the album Sweet Taste of Nothing, Oxygen Music Works (US), 1999) and soundscapes with Khim Efraimsson for the poetry concept Broken Word / Johannes Anyuru & Mercies May (the album Anatomy of a Dying Star, 2005). I have also made music for the Håkan Lidbo radio Ström concepts and compilations; What we are -music for alien civilizations, 2008 and Precycling Namibia -The Music From The Future, 2010.

The most recent full-length HAb album 'Why release music on plastic' for Kotangens records, Ukraine, was released in 2011. In 2012 HAb released the track Höst on Meerkat Recordings Nomads in Sound 03. The UNICEF-compilation project ’Born Into Madness’ 1, 2 & 3, M.O.L Music on location Belgium 2018, 2019, 2020. The compilation ’Australian bushfire’ Tuched music (UK) 2020. And finally, the Meerkat recordings 10-year anniversary jubilee compilation 2021.

What are your ideas behind your contribution to the Nomads in Sound anniversary compilation?
With the track Worlds (on fire), I believe that HAb wants to convey that not only life on this beautiful planet suffers from our irresponsible acts against nature, the oceans, the air, the animals, humanity, and the climate. Intergalactic life, other inner and outer worlds are also affected. In other words, we cause much more damage than we could even comprehend. We are in a paradigm shift, and I think HAb is here to help, comfort, perhaps raise our frequencies and consciousness.
