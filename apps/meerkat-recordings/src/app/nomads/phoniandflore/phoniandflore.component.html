How would you describe your musical background?
Being a drummer, I wanted to produce my own sounds using the ableton software. After meeting N-Tone Dub, we created the group The Outa Steppaz for a first date in Dordogne, which was my first live experience. A second fundamental encounter was with Sancho with whom I founded the Mar√©e BASS collective to promote underground music through parties and a record label.

This motivated me to launch my solo project Phoniandflore and produce reflective albums.

10 years since my debut, I have released 6 albums and some EPs on various labels. And almost a hundred appearances all over Europe at festivals, concerts, release parties, sound systems.

Encounters and enriching experiences which have, for sure, contributed to the development of my productions.

What is your main musical influence and/ or collective?
The discovery of Dub in 2000 -- through high tone, kaly live dub, brain damage, zenzile, lobe radiant, ezekiel and more -- was huge for me. It remains my favorite style of music ever since, but I listen to all kinds of music at home. Jazz, hip hop, world music, drum & bass, dubstep, techno, bass music.

Can you describe the main milestones in your earlier production/ projects?
My 6 albums have been created with very specific themes, each album took a year to complete.

The last 2 albums Into The Future and Ten Years Of Dub mark the evolution of the quality of my productions with a desire to mix dub sounds with other musical styles such as drum & bass and dubstep.

All these albums are of course free to download in order to share the music as much as possible.

Which are your current projects and what inspires you the most at the moment?
Following the release of the Ten Years Of Dub album, which celebrates the 10th anniversary of the Phoniandflore project, several projects are underway including a collaboration with MC JAZZZZ (hip hop dub project), a collaboration with Drop The Pulse and an EP Drum & Bass which will be released in 2021. In addition, The Outa Steppaz project is also back, we are producing a future release.

Which are your greatest passions as an artist?
Producing a new song and sharing it LIVE in public are my two favourite moments, which push me to continue and evolve. And album releases are always great highlights.

What are your ideas behind your contribution to the Nomads in Sound anniversary compilation?
The song I produced for the compilation is a mixture of influences from Arabic songs with some atmospheres from various countries in a nod to the term Nomads.
