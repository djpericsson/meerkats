How would you describe your musical background?
I started djing in the late 90s, and then began producing shortly after that. Early on i was making all tracks on an akai mpc 2000xl. I was at art college in Leeds with Dj Kidkanevil, he taught me the basics of how to use the mpc to sample and make beats. I have a degree in fine art and chose music technology as a side elective under Colin McGuire.

What is your main musical influence and/ or collective?
Sample based music has always been influential to me, digging for samples and using them creatively.

My main inspirations have been from the 90s Asian underground movement. I try to make music which is a development of that.

Can you describe the main milestones in your earlier production/ projects?
Having tunes played on the radio early on was motivating and inspiring.

Working and being mentored by artists like Ges-e, Osmani Soundz, Tony Thorpe and more recently John Rolodex have shaped my musical developent.

Which are your current projects and what inspires you the most at the moment?
I recently started a label along with some friends. The label is called Hundred Colours Music. The freedom to release music that I want is great.

Which are your greatest passions as an artist?
As well as music production I also enjoy oil painting.

I have a hectic and stressful work life, I feel lucky to have creative outlets in order to relax and de-stress.

What are your ideas behind your contribution to the Nomads in Sound anniversary compilation?
Like most of my tunes, I have contributed East-West flavoured tracks. Not too mild and not too spicy.
